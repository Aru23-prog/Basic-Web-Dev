Chicken Tandoori

Tandoori chicken is a dish made from chicken marinated in yogurt and spices and roasted in a tandoor, a cylindrical clay oven. The dish is now popular worldwide. The modern form of the dish was popularized by the Moti Mahal restaurant in New Delhi, India in the late 1940s.

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • Kashmiri Red Chili
  • Cumin
  • Garam masala
  • Coriander
  • Yogurt
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Lemon
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Combine yogurt, garlic, ginger, lemon juice, garam masala, ground coriander, ground cumin, chili powder, turmeric, salt and tomato paste in a large ceramic bowl.
  2. Cut 2 slits in top of each chicken thigh, making sure not to cut all the way through. Add to the yogurt mixture and turn to coat well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 3 hours.
  3. Preheat oven to 220Â°C. Grease and line a large baking dish. Place chicken in dish and roast for 30 minutes or until cooked through.
  4. Turn grill to HIGH and cook for 5-10 minutes or until chicken is charred. Sprinkle with parsley. Serve with rice and lemon.
